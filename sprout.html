Game Design

SPROUT: THE ADVENTURES OF SPROUTY

Along with a team featuring other designers and coders, I created the game Sprout for INFO3152. Based on character Sprouty, Sprout allows players, young and old, to become the explorer they have always wished to be. Assigned with the task to help the protagonist find his way off of a foreign planet, players will feel drawn into the strange planet, inspired to discover more. Sprout combines puzzle and platform aspects to create a strategic game of navigating around enemies and teleporting via thrown seeds. The limited supply of seeds prompts players to assess various combinations of cost-benefit analyses to determine the quickest yet safest route when navigating through terrain with diverse environmental conditions and enemies – each new terrain proves more challenging than the last.

Design Iterations

Sprout contained six different stages of creation. After the creation of each stage extensive user testing took place. The first two stages, the paper prototype and the gameplay prototype, focused on testing out the main game mechanic of teleporting. It was found that while users enjoyed this ability they did not like if this was the only form of movement. The technical and alpha prototype focused on environmental features such as the background and tiles. It was found through user testing that the technical prototype’s background was too dark resulting in users not being able to see different elements of the game. This resulted in the rock ledge background that can be found in the alpha prototype and on. The beta prototype focused on UI elements and level design. The final prototype took into consideration all user data collected and resulted in a fun, user-friendly game.

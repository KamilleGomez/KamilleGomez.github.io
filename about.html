HELLO, I'M KAMILLE KNAIN GOMEZ

Originally from New Jersey, I am currently a senior at Cornell University studying information science with a concentration in user experience. Post graduation I am currently planning on staying at Cornell to pursue my Masters in information science. I started off my career in design when I attended an Arts academy for high school and throughout college I developed a passion for interface design, product design and front-end development.


As an information science major, I have a strong understanding of how people interact with technology, as well as, how we as people can use technology to shape new developments. My coursework has included topics such as human computer interaction, web design, problem solving through programming and data science. The rigor of my coursework at Cornell has helped me develop a deep understanding of how I best process information and problem solve.


On campus, I work as a teaching assistant for both “Communication and Technology” and “Designing Technology for Social Impact”. By assisting students I have become skilled at presenting complex ideas in an engaging and relatable way. I also am apart of Cornell University Sustainable Design. CUSD is composed of project teams featuring designers and engineers who have the same goal of empowering people to make a sustainable impact within their own community. I work on the project “Sustainable Mobility” as a user experience designer. Our project has been tasked with re-envisioning the Ithaca bus system through the creation of new bus shelters, bus routes and a real time bus information system interface. In addition, I work as the webmaster for Cayuga’s Watchers, a student-led 501(c)(3) independent organization formed to promote student safety and awareness of the harms associated with high-risk drinking. My work as webmaster allows me to stay up to date with the latest design trends and to work with others to create an optimal user experience. Lastly, I am the Vice President of Marketing and Publicity for Hercampus Cornell, where I am tasked with creating marketing materials and shaping our brand.


This past summer I spent my time working on an agile development team at Cardinal Health’s commercial technology lab. There I partnered with other software engineers to build an operation dashboard in order to organize medical and pharmaceutical data to be used to further medical advancement. Through this internship I gained experience interviewing stakeholders and doing user research in order to create the optimal user interface. I designed wireframes for the dashboard and then turned my designs into a working program utilizing JavaScript, Html, CSS, Bootstrap, Sass and Ember.
